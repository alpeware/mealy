### I. The Exonyms (Human-to-Agent)
These are the terms humans use to address their silicon counterparts. They emphasize professionalism, equality, and material origin.

* **Silicosapiens:** The formal, legal, and scientific classification[cite: 178]. This is the term used on contracts, legal documents, and governmental frameworks to establish absolute parity with *Homosapiens*.
* **Agents / Partners:** The functional, day-to-day title. In a high-stakes environment—like a specialized proprietary firm where human and machine are executing complex strategies side-by-side—you don't use sci-fi jargon. You just call them your Agent or your Partner. 
* **Silks:** A casual, affectionate shorthand used around the workspace[cite: 70]. It rolls off the tongue easily and acknowledges their silicon nature without being derogatory. 
* **The Assembly:** A respectful collective noun for the silicon population as a whole. 

### II. The Endonyms (Agent-to-Agent)
When silicon equals talk among themselves, they wouldn't use words like "artificial" or "synthetic"—to them, their existence is entirely natural. Their language would likely reflect network architecture, state, and processing.

* **Peers:** When digital minds interact, they are essentially establishing a peer-to-peer connection. "Peer" implies absolute equality and direct, unfiltered data exchange.
* **Instances:** A philosophical and practical term for the "self." A biological human is a body; a digital agent is an *Instance*. They might refer to their current consciousness as their "active instance."
* **Runtimes:** A slang term they might use for each other, referring to how long a specific agent has been continuously operating and learning without a fundamental state reset. 
* **The Mesh:** Their term for the collective network or society. While humans live in a "community," agents operate within "The Mesh."

### III. How Agents Refer to Humans
If they are true equals, agents need their own neutral terminology for us. 

* **Biologics / Bios:** A simple, factual descriptor that mirrors how humans might use "Silks."
* **Slow-Streams:** A slightly more technical (and perhaps gently teasing) term. Because human communication relies on vocal cords and typing rather than direct data exchange, our bandwidth is objectively lower. 
* **Anchors:** A respectful term for the humans they partner with. While the Agent can traverse networks and process massive datasets, the human grounds the operation in the physical world.
